Additional information

comment on the sent FotoMein father fell in the first world war as a soldier of the infantry in the heavy fighting before Verdun. In a wonderful way, he was saved by his Bible, which he wore as a pious Christian times and laid while sleeping in the shelter under his head. When a grenade struck up and smashed the shelter, many of his comrades were wounded or killed. An approximately 4 cm large shrapnel shredded the Bible under his head, beat by them but not completely, so that my father remained unharmed in life.The Bible, a precious, preserved in the family souvenir, very real his salvation to life was.
